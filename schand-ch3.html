Question 5:
David invests Rs. 6250 in shares of a company paying 6% per annum. If he bought Rs. 25 shares for Rs. 31.25 each, find his annual income from this investment.

By investing Rs. 31.25, shares can be purchased = 1
By investing Rs. 6250, number of shares can be purchased = 6250 ÷ 31.25 = 200
Nominal value of 200 shares at the rate of Rs. 25 per share = Rs. 25 × 200 = Rs. 5000
Rate of dividend = 6%
Total dividend = Rs. 5000 × 6/100 = Rs. 300

Question 7:
Mukul invests Rs. 9,000 in a company paying dividend of 6% per annum when a share of face value 100 stands at Rs. 150. What is his annual income? He sells 50% of his shares when the price rises to Rs. 200. What is his gain?
Investment = Rs. 9,000
Dividend = 6%
Face value = Rs. 100; Market value (buying) = Rs. 150
Shares bought = 9,000/150 = 60
Annual dividend = Rs. 100 × 60 × 6/100 = Rs. 360
Amount received by selling 30 shares at Rs. 200 = Rs. 6,000
Market value (selling) = Rs. 200
Amount received on remaining 30 shares at Rs. 150 = Rs. 4,500
Total received = Rs. 6,000 + 4,500 = Rs. 10,500
Gain = 10,500 - 9,000 = Rs. 1,500

Question 20:
A person invested 20%, 30% and 25% of his savings in buying shares of three different companies, A, B, C, which declared dividends of 10%, 12%, 15% respectively. Total income on account of dividends is Rs. 2,337.50, find savings and amount invested in each.
Let savings = x.
0.2x × 10/100 + 0.3x × 12/100 + 0.25x × 15/100 = 2,337.5
0.02x + 0.036x + 0.0375x = 0.0935x = 2,337.5
x = 2,337.5 / 0.0935 = Rs. 25,000
In A: 0.2 × 25,000 = Rs. 5,000; In B: 0.3 × 25,000 = Rs. 7,500; In C: 0.25 × 25,000 = Rs. 6,250
