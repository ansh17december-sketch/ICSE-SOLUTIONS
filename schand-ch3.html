Question 5:
David invests Rs. 6250 in shares of a company paying 6% per annum. If he bought Rs. 25 shares for Rs. 31.25 each, find his annual income from this investment.

By investing Rs. 31.25, shares can be purchased = 1
By investing Rs. 6250, number of shares can be purchased = 6250 ÷ 31.25 = 200
Nominal value of 200 shares at the rate of Rs. 25 per share = Rs. 25 × 200 = Rs. 5000
Rate of dividend = 6%
Total dividend = Rs. 5000 × 6/100 = Rs. 300

Question 7:
A company declares semi-annual dividend of 5%. Krishna Lal owns 25 shares of par value Rs. 12.50 each. How much annual dividend must he receive?

Face value of each share = Rs. 12.50
Value of 25 shares = Rs. 12.50 × 25 = Rs. 312.50

Annual dividend = 5 × 2 = 10%
Total dividend = Rs. 312.50 × 10/100 = Rs. 31.25

Question 9:
A man invests Rs. 4800 in shares of a company which was paying 8% dividend at the time when a Rs. 100 share cost Rs. 160. Find (i) his annual income from the shares, (ii) the rate of interest he gets on his investment.

Investment = Rs. 4800
Rate of dividend = 8%
Face value of each share = Rs. 100
Market value of each share = Rs. 160
Number of shares = 4800 ÷ 160 = 30
(i) Total face value of 30 shares = Rs. 100 × 30 = Rs. 3000
(ii) Rate of interest on his investment = (240/4800) × 100 = 5%
Question 10:
A man bought 1000 shares each of face value Rs. 5 at Rs. 7 per share. At the end of the year, the company from which he bought the shares declared a dividend of 8%. Calculate:
(i) the amount of money invested by the man.
(ii) the percentage return on his outlay correct to one decimal place.

Number of shares = 1000
Nominal value of each share = Rs. 5
Market value of each share = Rs. 7
Rate of dividend = 8%
(i) Total face value of 1000 shares = Rs. 5 × 1000 = Rs. 5000
Amount of investment = 1000 × Rs. 7 = Rs. 7000
(ii) Total dividend = Rs. 5000 × 8/100 = Rs. 400
Rate of interest = Rs. (400/7000) × 100 = 5.7%
Percentage of return = 5.7%
Question 11:
A company with 10000 shares of nominal value Rs. 100 declares an annual dividend of 8% to the share-holders.
Calculate the total amount of dividend paid by the company.
(i) Ramesh had bought 90 shares of the company at Rs. 150 per share. What percentage return does he get on his investment?

Number of shares = 10000
Nominal value of each share = Rs. 100
Rate of dividend = 8%
Total face value of all shares = Rs. 100 × 10000 = Rs. 1000000
(i) Total dividend paid by the company = Rs. 1000000 × 8/100 = Rs. 80000
Number of shares Ramesh has = 90
Value of 90 shares = Rs. 100 × 90 = Rs. 9000
Dividend = Rs. 9000 × 8/100 = Rs. 720
Market value per share = Rs. 150
Investment cost = 90 shares × Rs. 150 × 90 = Rs. 13500
Percent of interest on his investment = (720/13500) × 100 = 5.3%
Question 12:

(i) the sale proceeds
(ii) the number of shares she buys
(iii) the annual dividend on these shares.

Nominal value of each share = Rs. 100

(i) Amount received on selling them = Rs. 150 × 40 = Rs. 6000
(ii) In second case, nominal value of each share = Rs. 75
Market value of each share 20% discount = Rs. 75 - 15 = Rs. 60
Number of shares purchased = 6000 ÷ 60 = 100
Rate of dividend = 20% annually
(iii) Number of shares = 100
(iv) Face value of 100 shares at the rate of Rs. 75 = 75 × 100 = Rs. 7500
(v) Annual dividend = Rs. 7500 × 20/100 = Rs. 1875
Question 13:
Mr. Khanna holds 1600, Rs. 100 shares of a company that pays 12% dividend annually. Calculate his annual dividend. If he sells these shares at 30% premium, what percentage return does he get on this investment?

Number of shares Mr. Khanna holds = 1600
Face value of each share = Rs. 100
Rate of dividend = 12%
(i) Total value of 1600 shares = Rs. 100 × 1600 = Rs. 160000
Total dividend = Rs. 160000 × 12/100 = Rs. 19200
Cost per share = Rs. 100 + 50 = Rs. 150
Total investment = 1600 × 150 = Rs. 240000
Percentage return will be = (19200/240000) × 100 = 8%
Question 14:
A man invests Rs. 11200 in a company paying 5% dividend per annum when the Rs. 100 shares can be bought for Rs. 140. Find (i) his annual dividend, (ii) his percentage income on this investment.

Total investment = Rs. 11200
Rate of dividend = 5%
Face value of each share = Rs. 100
Market value = Rs. 140
Number of shares purchased = 11200 ÷ 140 = 80
(i) Annual dividend = Rs. 8000 × 5/100 = Rs. 400
(ii) Percentage income on his investment = (400/11200) × 100 = 3.57%
